Psychology Reference
Big Five (OCEAN) Explained
Openness — Intellectual curiosity, creativity, appreciation for new experiences. High = imaginative. Low = prefers routine.

Conscientiousness — Organization, dependability, self-discipline. High = reliable and goal-directed. Low = spontaneous but may lack follow-through.

Extraversion — Sociability, assertiveness, positive emotions. High = gains energy from others. Low (introvert) = recharges alone.

Agreeableness — Cooperativeness, empathy, trust. High = warm and conflict-averse. Low = competitive or skeptical.

Neuroticism — Emotional instability, anxiety, vulnerability. High = reacts more intensely to stress. Not a character flaw — a measure of emotional sensitivity.
Attachment Theory Primer
Bowlby & Ainsworth: early caregiver responses create internal templates for adult relationships.

Secure — Comfortable with intimacy and autonomy. Can ask for needs directly. Resilient after conflict.
Anxious-Preoccupied — Craves closeness, fears abandonment. Hyperactivates attachment system. May come across as needy.
Dismissive-Avoidant — Values independence. Deactivates attachment system. May seem cold or self-contained.
Fearful-Avoidant — Desires and fears closeness simultaneously. Linked to early relational trauma.

Attachment patterns can shift toward security through therapy, self-awareness, and safe relationships.
Dark Triad — What It Means
Three overlapping traits. All humans have some degree of each. Elevated combinations are associated with exploitative behavior.

Narcissism — Grandiosity, entitlement, limited empathy. Distinguished from healthy confidence by intolerance of criticism.

Machiavellianism — Strategic manipulation, self-interest prioritization, cynical worldview.

Psychopathy — Callousness, poor impulse control, shallow affect. Note: subclinical psychopathy is not criminality.

MirrorMind scores are probabilistic text estimates — not clinical diagnosis.
Love Languages — Full Guide
Words of Affirmation — Verbal appreciation, compliments, encouragement. Affected deeply by harsh words.

Acts of Service — Doing helpful things. Actions speak louder than words. Broken promises are deeply hurtful.

Receiving Gifts — Thoughtful gifts signal "I was thinking of you." Absence of gifts can feel like neglect.

Quality Time — Undivided attention and presence. Phone distraction during their time is a deep wound.

Physical Touch — Hugs, hand-holding, proximity. Touch communicates safety more powerfully than words.
Nigerian & African Cultural Intelligence
Collectivism: Identity rooted in family, community, lineage. Decisions, criticism, and love expression are all filtered through collective belonging.

Face-Saving: Public humiliation is one of the most damaging acts in high-context cultures. Criticism must be private and indirect. Praise should be public.

Hierarchy & Respect: Age, wealth, title, and lineage confer authority. Challenging them publicly reads as disrespect, not principle.

Indirect Communication: Many things said through proverbs, stories, or third-party framing. Directness can be read as aggression.

Code-Switching: Urban Nigerians and diaspora Africans move fluidly between formal English, Pidgin, and indigenous languages — each carrying distinct relational meaning.
